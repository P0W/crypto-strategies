# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* **Being respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
* **Being collaborative**: Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
* **Being empathetic**: Showing empathy toward other community members
* **Being professional**: Focusing on what is best for the community
* **Being patient**: Understanding that people have different learning curves
* **Giving credit**: Acknowledging the contributions of others

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* Spamming issues or pull requests
* Dismissing or attacking inclusion-focused requests
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Trading-Specific Guidelines

Given this is a trading strategy repository:

* **No financial advice**: This is educational/research software. Don't provide personal financial advice.
* **Honest results**: Report backtest results honestly. Don't cherry-pick or mislead.
* **Risk disclosure**: Always mention risk when discussing strategies.
* **No pump and dump**: Don't coordinate market manipulation.
* **Respect IP**: Don't share proprietary strategies without permission.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and wikis
* Code comments and documentation
* Community chat channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)
* Social media when representing the project
* In-person events or meetups

It also applies when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of
acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
* Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions
* Any content that violates this Code of Conduct

They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting Issues

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **GitHub**: Create a private issue (if feature available)
2. **Email**: Contact project maintainer directly
3. **Direct Message**: Reach out to maintainers on community platforms

### What to Include

Please include in your report:

* Your contact information
* Names (usernames) of people involved
* Description of the incident
* Links to relevant content
* Any additional context

### Confidentiality

All reports will be:
* Kept confidential
* Reviewed by maintainers
* Taken seriously
* Responded to promptly

Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Minor inappropriate behavior or unprofessional language.

**Consequence**: Private written warning, providing clarity around the violation
and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may
be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: Warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction
with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding
interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms
may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: Serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: Temporary ban from any interaction or public communication
with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction
with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms
may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation, sustained harassment,
or aggression toward individuals or groups.

**Consequence**: Permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

## Appeals

If you believe you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code
of Conduct, you should notify the project maintainers with a concise description
of your grievance. Your appeal will be reviewed and investigated in accordance
with the same guidelines.

## Community Expectations

### For Contributors

* **Be patient with beginners**: Everyone starts somewhere
* **Provide constructive feedback**: Focus on improvement
* **Share knowledge**: Help others learn
* **Document your work**: Make it easy for others to understand
* **Test your code**: Don't break existing functionality

### For Maintainers

* **Be responsive**: Acknowledge contributions promptly
* **Be fair**: Apply standards consistently
* **Be transparent**: Explain decisions clearly
* **Be supportive**: Encourage new contributors
* **Be protective**: Foster a safe environment

### For Users

* **Search before asking**: Check existing issues and docs
* **Provide details**: Help us help you with complete info
* **Be respectful of time**: Maintainers are volunteers
* **Give back**: Share improvements and bug fixes
* **Report issues responsibly**: Follow security disclosure process

## Positive Community Building

We encourage:

* **Welcoming newcomers**: Help them get started
* **Celebrating contributions**: Acknowledge good work
* **Learning together**: Share knowledge and experiences
* **Constructive debate**: Disagree respectfully
* **Continuous improvement**: Iterate on both code and community
